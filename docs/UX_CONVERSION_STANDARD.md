# UX Conversion Standard

Every customer-facing page should answer: what is this, who is it for, what do I get, when should I use it, what happens next, what does this not do, why can I trust this, and what should I click next.

Required sections: Hero, Trust Bar, Short Answer, Who This Is For, Product or Guide Value, What You Get, How It Works, Use Cases, FAQ, What This Does Not Do, Next Steps, Why ApprovalPrep, Final CTA, Boundary Notice, and Footer.

Every useful page may include a CTA. Index status controls search exposure, not whether a page can sell. CTAs must be honest, relevant, plain, and safe.
## Free Studio Funnel

The homepage must offer a clear `$0` starting path before paid kit selection. The Letter Writing Studio is a free, browser-only guided drafting tool that helps uncertain users organize facts, create a first-pass draft, and identify the right paid kit. It must not call AI APIs, upload files, store answers, create accounts, or create marginal vendor cost.

## Navigation Standard

Top navigation must act like a buying guide: Products, Letter Studio, Use Cases, Pricing, Resources, and Start Here. The mobile nav must not overflow, clip, or bury the primary `$0` starting path.

## Commercial CTA Rhythm

Commercial pages need a hero CTA, mid-page fit CTA, included-offerings CTA, post-objection CTA, and final CTA. The path must support both uncertain users who should start with the free Studio and ready buyers who should proceed to checkout.

## Paid Product Delivery Model

Every paid ApprovalPrep product is a direct paid download. The free Studio can help a user draft, orient, and choose a kit, but it is never required to buy or use a paid product. After verified Stripe checkout, the buyer unlocks the purchased kit PDF guide and editable DOCX file.
