# UX Conversion Standard

Every customer-facing page should answer: what is this, who is it for, what do I get, when should I use it, what happens next, what does this not do, why can I trust this, and what should I click next.

Required sections: Hero, Trust Bar, Short Answer, Who This Is For, Product or Guide Value, What You Get, How It Works, Use Cases, FAQ, What This Does Not Do, Next Steps, Why ApprovalPrep, Final CTA, Boundary Notice, and Footer.

Every useful page may include a CTA. Index status controls search exposure, not whether a page can sell. CTAs must be honest, relevant, plain, and safe.
## Free Studio Funnel

The homepage must offer a clear `$0` starting path for uncertain users, but the paid product model must remain primary and clear. The Letter Writing Studio is a free, browser-only guided drafting tool that helps uncertain users organize facts, create a first-pass draft, and identify the right paid kit. It must not call AI APIs, upload files, store answers, create accounts, or create marginal vendor cost. It must not be presented as required before purchase.

## Navigation Standard

Top navigation must act like a buying guide: Products, Letter Studio, Pricing, Resources, and a clear pricing CTA. Use cases must live in a visible secondary intent rail with the important subpages laid out directly, including DIY credit letters, explanation letters, apartment readiness, income and job proof, loan readiness, business funding, life-admin paperwork, and the full bundle. The primary nav must not use `Start Here $0`.

## Homepage Positioning

The homepage must sell the strongest compliant commercial promise first: self-service credit repair letters and approval-ready document kits at a lower-cost, direct-download model. It can use phrases such as "self-service credit repair letters," "DIY credit letters," and "without the high monthly fees" when paired elsewhere on the page and site with truthful self-service boundaries. Do not lead the hero with negative warning copy such as "no credit repair." Boundaries belong in trust/legal sections, footer, and product detail context, not in the first conversion moment.

## Commercial CTA Rhythm

Commercial pages need a hero CTA, mid-page fit CTA, included-offerings CTA, post-objection CTA, and final CTA. The path must support both uncertain users who should start with the free Studio and ready buyers who should proceed to checkout.

## Paid Product Delivery Model

Every paid ApprovalPrep product is a direct paid download. The free Studio can help a user draft, orient, and choose a kit, but it is never required to buy or use a paid product. After verified Stripe checkout, the buyer unlocks the purchased kit PDF guide and editable DOCX file.
